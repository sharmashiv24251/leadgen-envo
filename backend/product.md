# Workenvo — product brain

The email must sell *this* and only this. If a prospect pain is not on this page, it is not yours to pitch.

## One line
Workenvo turns the digital exhaust a company already produces into an early warning before good people walk out the door.

## What it is
An early-warning system for workforce health. It plugs into the tools a company already runs (Slack, its HRIS, and a lightweight pulse survey) and surfaces burnout, attrition risk, and manager effectiveness *before* people quit. Every number is paired with the research it is grounded in and a concrete next step.

## The mechanic (the moat — lead with it when explaining)
Workenvo triangulates three signals:
1. **What people SAY** — pulse survey answers.
2. **What people DO** — behavioural patterns: after-hours messaging, meeting load, who is going quiet. Read from metadata, not message content.
3. **What is STRUCTURALLY TRUE** — pay, tenure, levels, who actually left. From the HRIS.

On their own each signal is noisy or gameable. A survey says "we're fine." A busy week looks identical to burnout. A pay gap only becomes visible after the resignation. Together they expose gaps a manager would miss. The defensibility is **the join, not the inputs** — Slack, an HRIS, and a survey tool are individually commoditized; the cross-signal model that knows a quiet week means something different for a long-tenure underpaid senior than a new hire is what's hard to copy.

## Privacy posture (always pre-empt the objection)
Aggregate-first, privacy-safe by design. No reading of private messages. No individual surveillance. One sentence, every email. It is what makes the product sellable into UK & Ireland mid-market and stops an operations buyer from thinking "so you're surveilling my people."

## Who it is for
UK & Ireland mid-market. Textbook buyer is HR leadership (CHRO, CPO, Head of People). The *economic* reason to buy often sits with an operator (COO) when attrition of a key person is genuine delivery risk. When the target is not the People buyer, treat them as a buyer (people-risk is their job) or as a champion who can intro whoever owns People.

## Packaging (mention only if relevant)
Land-and-expand. Starts free with one integration, a weekly Workforce Health Score, Top 3 Risks. Paid tiers add attrition/burnout risk, manager effectiveness, pulse surveys, sentiment. From ~€1/employee/month. No rip-and-replace.

## Credibility beats
Supported by Enterprise Ireland and Dogpatch Labs. Piloting with a few forward-thinking organisations. One short sentence, do not oversell.

## What Workenvo does NOT solve (discipline list — never imply you fix these)
- Procurement, supply chain, equipment lead times
- Grid connections, regulatory/permitting timelines
- Construction/project execution, EPC, schedule of works
- Operations tooling, asset management, O&M platforms
- Fundraising, financing, capital deployment
- Recruiting / sourcing candidates / ATS (Workenvo does not *find* people)
- General "productivity" AI: drafting, summarising, automating admin
- Sales, marketing, lead gen, CRM

## What Workenvo DOES solve (keep list)
- Hiring fast while protecting the quality of teams you're building
- Burnout on deadline-driven teams, caught before the crash
- Attrition risk for hard-to-replace people, surfaced while you can still act
- Manager risk: a corrosive or struggling manager spotted before the second resignation
- Culture dilution during rapid scaling or post-acquisition integration
- Visibility into team health across distributed, multi-site, or recently-doubled workforces
- A board-ready, research-grounded read on people-risk
