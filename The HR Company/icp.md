# The HR Company ICP (v1 — edit this file to retarget; the runner re-reads it every run)

Find a person who fits the profile below, at a company where you can find at least one true,
recent, specific thing to open the email with. The hook is a conversational opener, not a
qualifying event. Source doc: "The HR Company ICP Defined Jul 26."

## Who (the company) — primary ICP

- **Geography:** Ireland only. (UK is a possible future expansion — not yet.)
- **Size:** 10–100 employees. Below 10, issues are infrequent and price sensitivity is high.
  Above 100, internal HR already exists or is being built — not the target.
- **Maturity:** 5+ years trading, past the "startup chaos" phase, growing headcount or
  operational complexity, informal HR practices starting to break.
- **Industry, in priority order:**
  1. Construction & engineering contractors
  2. Care & healthcare providers
  3. GP practices
  4. Aesthetic clinics
  5. Physiotherapy & consultant-led clinics
  6. Logistics, transport & warehousing
  7. Manufacturing & light industrial
  8. Hospitality (multi-site or high headcount)
  9. Independent retailers (not owned by Musgrave)

## Who (the company) — secondary ICP

Newly incorporated Irish companies that need HR support set up from day one (contracts,
working time rules, minimum wage — Irish employment law has strong protections that apply
from the first day of employment). Priority: companies registered in Ireland, or relocated to
Ireland from the US, since **January 2026**.

## The person

Primary buyer (any of these can decide, not a strict hierarchy):
- Owner / Managing Director / Founder
- Operations Director / General Manager
- HR Manager / HR Director (if one already exists but is stretched or junior)

Influencers (write to these as a champion ask, not a direct buyer ask):
- Finance Manager
- Practice Manager (clinics)
- Site / Project Manager (construction)

## Disqualify

- Micro-businesses (under 10 employees)
- Startups / founders hiring their first employees
- Price-led buyers who only want template documents, not advisory
- Businesses with a strong internal HR team already
- Companies that only want an annual policy update, nothing ongoing
- Anyone already on the contacted list (`wk-check-contacted` handles this)

## Hook material to look for (opener fuel, NOT gates)

Trigger moments are mostly private (a bad disciplinary, a WRC notice) and won't show up in a
web search — don't go looking for those directly. Look instead for the externally **visible**
signals that correlate with them:

- Job postings for an HR Manager / HR Admin / People & Culture role — the clearest signal a
  company has outgrown informal HR and is actively trying to solve it (whether via hiring
  or, potentially, outsourcing instead).
- A recent funding round, contract win, or expansion announcement driving a hiring surge —
  headcount growing faster than process.
- A new office, site, or depot opening (especially multi-site operators — construction,
  logistics, hospitality, healthcare groups).
- Public **Workplace Relations Commission (WRC)** case records — genuinely public record in
  Ireland, and directly on-ICP if the company or a near-identical peer in the same sector
  shows up.
- Newer companies (secondary ICP): recent Companies Registration Office incorporation date,
  or public news of a US company relocating/expanding into Ireland, since Jan 2026.
- Recruitment posts or forum questions (LinkedIn, Reddit, industry forums) asking basic HR
  questions — a company thinking out loud about a problem they haven't solved yet.

If you genuinely cannot find a single true, specific hook, skip the prospect and find another.
A generic opener ("saw you're growing") is worse than no email.
